Research Proposal: Brain Foundation Models

Foundation models have transformed fields like natural language processing and computer vision, offering unparalleled generalization across tasks and datasets. Yet, their potential in neuroscience remains untapped. While foundation models (such as transformers pretrained on large volumes of data) do not directly provide simple and interpretable models of brain function, their capacity for capturing complex and high-dimensional representations positions them as powerful tools for advancing medicine and neuroscience. This research aims to leverage these models to achieve a deeper understanding of brain dynamics, enabling transformative applications in neural simulation, precise modulation of brain activity, and improved brain-computer interfaces.

The envisioned future includes a comprehensive computational model of the brain, where the brain state can be decoded, simulated, and interfaced with seamlessly. Through invasive and non-invasive interventions, these models could facilitate tailored treatments for neurological disorders such as epilepsy, enhance communication between brains and machines as well as between brains and other brains, and optimize stimulation protocols for brain control and repair. By aligning the development of foundation models with neural data, this work aims to unlock the potential for predictive, interpretable, and scalable neural interventions that could redefine our interaction with and understanding of the human brain. Such advancements would pave the way for neural prosthetics that seamlessly integrate with the brain, simulate or restore cognitive functions, and improve quality of life for individuals with neurological disorders.

This vision aligns with a broader aspiration: to build tools that merge great computational power with biological reality, laying the groundwork for an alternative path to understanding and transforming the human brain.
